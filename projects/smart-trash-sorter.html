Smart Home Trash Sorter

IoT Product Design • Automation • Sustainable Technology

An extension of the LitterMaster app concept that asks: "Why bother users with sorting trash when the bin can do it automatically?" This smart home solution combines computer vision, mechanical engineering, and IoT connectivity to create an autonomous waste sorting system that eliminates user effort while maximizing recycling efficiency.

Initial Thesis

The core insight driving this project: if we can teach phones to recognize faces, why can't we teach trash bins to recognize recyclables?

Design Process

Hand sketches exploring mechanical sorting mechanisms, user interaction flows, and internal component layout for the three-compartment system.

Initial concept sketches showing sorting mechanism and compartment design

3D Design Evolution

Three iterative prototypes exploring different approaches to automated sorting, form factor optimization, and user accessibility.

Final Design

Sleek cylindrical form with three hidden compartments and top-mounted vision system. This design incorporates user feedback from the previous concepts, featuring an intuitive interface and optimized sorting mechanism for maximum efficiency.

Design Philosophy

This project represents the natural evolution of waste management technology. While the LitterMaster app educated users about proper sorting, the Smart Home Trash Sorter eliminates the need for user education entirely.

By automating the sorting process at the point of disposal, we remove human error, increase recycling rates, and make sustainable living effortless. The corded design ensures reliability—because sustainability shouldn't depend on remembering to charge your trash can.
